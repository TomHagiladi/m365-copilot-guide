What Is Microsoft 365 Copilot?

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an AI assistant powered by large language models (GPT-4 and above, including the new GPT-5.2) embedded directly into the Microsoft 365 apps you already use — Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, Teams, and more. It operates within your organization's security and privacy boundaries and can access your files, emails, meetings, and chats to provide contextual, relevant assistance.

💬 Copilot Chat

A central chat interface that connects all your organizational data — files, emails, meetings, and web search. Your AI command center for work.

📝 Embedded in Apps

Copilot is built directly into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams — it works on the file you have open and understands its context.

🤖 AI Agents

Advanced reasoning agents like Researcher and Analyst perform multi-step research and complex data analysis — new in 2025!

🔒 Security & Privacy

Copilot operates within your organizational permissions — it only accesses data you're authorized to see and does not use your data for model training.

ℹ️

Academic Licensing: Microsoft 365 Copilot is available for academic institutions at $18/user/month (faculty, staff, and students 13+). Additionally, Copilot Chat (basic version) is available at no cost for users with a Microsoft 365 A1, A3, or A5 license.

The Art of Prompting — How to Communicate with Copilot

A great prompt is the key to great results. Microsoft recommends a four-element framework for effective prompting. You don't need all four every time — but the more specific you are, the better the output.

The Effective Prompt Formula

🎯 Goal
What to do
+
📋 Context
Why & for whom
+
📂 Source
Which files/data
+
📏 Expectations
Format & tone

🎯 Goal

What do you want Copilot to do? This is the only required element. Examples: "Summarize," "Write," "Analyze," "Compare," "Create a table."

📋 Context

Why do you need this? Who is the audience? Example: "I'm writing a grant proposal for a science foundation," "For a journal article submission."

📂 Source

What materials should Copilot use? Files, emails, meetings. Use / to reference a specific file or SharePoint site.

📏 Expectations

How should the response look? Length, format, style, tone. Example: "In a table format," "Under 200 words," "Formal academic tone."

💡

Important Tip: Word order matters in prompts! Microsoft notes that the latter parts of a prompt tend to receive more emphasis. If you want Copilot to use a specific file, put the file reference at the end of your prompt.

💡

Persona Prompting: Try assigning a role to Copilot. For example: "As an expert research methodologist..." — this helps steer the tone, depth, and angle of the response.

⚠️

Always verify the output! Copilot is an assistant, not a replacement for professional judgment. Check factual accuracy, citations, and sources before using any generated content.

Copilot Chat — Your Command Center

Copilot Chat (accessible at microsoft365.com/copilot or the M365 app) is the central chat interface. In Work mode, it accesses all your organizational data. In Web mode, it searches the internet. Reference specific files using /.

Key Capabilities

Summarize emails & files | Search organizational data | Generate content | Answer questions based on org documents | Web search | Create Word/Excel/PowerPoint files from chat | Access SharePoint via / | Model selector with GPT-5.2

Prompt — Organizational Summary
Summarize all emails I received this week about [research project name] and list the key action items and deadlines mentioned.
Prompt — Document Search
Find all documents in our SharePoint that are related to [topic] and provide a brief summary of each one, highlighting the most recent updates.
Prompt — Meeting Preparation
Help me prepare for my meeting with [name/team] tomorrow. Summarize our recent email exchanges, any shared documents, and the notes from our last meeting.
Prompt — Create a Document from Chat
Create a Word document with a literature review outline on [topic]. Include sections for: introduction, theoretical framework, methodology review, key findings, and gaps in the literature.
💡

New! You can now search your Copilot conversation history, including chats from Notebooks, Agents, and the main interface — all through an integrated search experience.

Copilot in Word — Academic Writing

Copilot in Word helps you create, rewrite, summarize, and edit documents. It understands the context of the open document and can reference other files. The new Agent Mode allows Copilot to make multi-step edits across the entire document.

Key Capabilities

Draft content from scratch | Rewrite & rephrase | Summarize long documents | Add citations and references | Ask questions about content | Convert text to tables | Agent Mode for multi-step editing

Prompt — Research Abstract
Write an academic abstract (250 words max) for a research paper about [topic]. Follow the structure: background, objective, methods, key findings, and conclusion. Use formal academic tone.
Prompt — Improve Academic Writing
Rewrite the selected paragraph to improve academic clarity and flow. Maintain the original meaning but use more precise terminology and stronger transitions. Keep a formal scholarly tone.
Prompt — Structured Summary
Summarize this document in a structured format: (1) Research question, (2) Methodology, (3) Key findings, (4) Limitations, (5) Implications. Keep each section to 2-3 sentences.
Prompt — Literature Review Draft
Based on /[filename], draft a literature review section that synthesizes the key themes, identifies areas of consensus and debate, and highlights gaps that warrant further research.
Prompt — Research Proposal
Draft a research proposal outline for a study on [topic]. Include: title, research questions, significance, theoretical framework, methodology, expected outcomes, timeline, and budget. Target: academic grant committee.
Prompt — Agent Mode (Full Document Edit)
Review this entire document and improve it: fix grammatical errors, enhance clarity, ensure consistent citation formatting, add transition sentences between sections, and create a table of contents. Show me each change.
💡

Citations: Copilot in Word can add in-text citations and a references list. Always verify the accuracy of every citation it generates!

Copilot in Excel — Data Analysis

Copilot in Excel helps create formulas, analyze data, identify trends, and present insights as charts and Pivot Tables. New features include the =COPILOT() function, Python support, and Agent Mode for multi-step tasks.

Key Capabilities

Natural language formulas | Data analysis & insights | Charts & Pivot Tables | =COPILOT() function | Python analysis | Agent Mode | Works with local files

Prompt — Statistical Analysis
Analyze this dataset: provide descriptive statistics (mean, median, standard deviation) for all numerical columns, identify outliers, and highlight significant trends.
Prompt — Create a Chart
Create a chart showing the relationship between [column A] and [column B]. Use a scatter plot with a trend line and clear axis labels.
Prompt — Formulas
Add a new column that calculates the percentage change between [column name] values from one row to the next, and highlight changes greater than 10%.
Prompt — Survey Data Analysis
Analyze this survey data: calculate response frequencies, create cross-tabulations between [demographic variable] and [response variable], and identify statistically significant patterns.
Prompt — Python Analysis
Using Python, perform a correlation analysis between all numerical columns. Generate a correlation matrix heatmap and highlight the strongest correlations.
Prompt — =COPILOT() Example
=COPILOT("Classify the sentiment of this text as Positive, Negative, or Neutral", A2)
ℹ️

New — =COPILOT(): AI directly in your formulas. Enter a natural language prompt, reference cells, and get AI results that update automatically when data changes.

💡

Agent Mode in Excel: Plans, executes, and validates multi-step tasks — building models, reshaping tables, creating charts — directly in the grid.

Copilot in PowerPoint — Academic Presentations

Copilot in PowerPoint creates full presentations, designs slides, adds images, and summarizes existing decks. In the new Agent Mode, you can create and edit presentations through natural conversation.
Prompt — Conference Presentation
Create a 12-slide presentation for an academic conference about [topic]. Include: title slide, background, literature review, methodology, results, discussion, limitations, future research, and references.
Prompt — Presentation from Document
Create a presentation based on /[research_paper.docx]. Extract key points, organize logically across slides, include main findings. Keep text concise — max 4 bullet points per slide.
Prompt — Lecture Slides
Create a lecture presentation on [topic] for graduate students. Include explanations, examples, and discussion questions. Add speaker notes with talking points for each slide.
Prompt — Redesign & Restructure
Reorganize this presentation to improve narrative flow. Add transition slides, make text more concise, and suggest where visual elements would strengthen the message.
💡

Reference files: Use / to point Copilot to a Word document, PDF, or another presentation as source material — perfect for turning papers into conference slides.

Copilot in Outlook — Communication Management

Copilot in Outlook helps draft emails, summarize long threads, and manage your inbox intelligently. New features include attachment search, smart auto-replies matching your style, and voice experience on mobile.
Prompt — Academic Email
Draft a professional email to [journal editor / conference organizer / collaborator] regarding [topic]. Formal academic tone. Include: greeting, purpose, key details, and a clear call to action.
Prompt — Thread Summary
Summarize this email thread. Key decisions, open questions, and action items? Who needs to do what by when?
Prompt — Peer Review Response
Help me draft a response to this peer review feedback. For each point, acknowledge the concern, explain how we addressed it (or why we disagree), and reference the specific changes made.
Prompt — Adjust Tone
Rewrite this email to be more [formal / concise / diplomatic / encouraging]. Keep the core message but adjust the tone for [audience].

Copilot in Teams — Meetings & Collaboration

Copilot in Teams summarizes meetings in real time, tracks decisions and action items, and provides customizable recap templates. New: Speaker Summary, Executive Summary, custom templates, Audio Recap, and chat search.
Prompt — Meeting Summary
Summarize this meeting. Main topics, decisions, and action items? Organize by topic and indicate who is responsible.
Prompt — Research Meeting Tracking
What research-related decisions were made? List methodological changes, timeline updates, and assigned tasks.
Prompt — Real-Time Question
What has been discussed about [specific topic]? Did anyone raise concerns or suggest alternatives?
Prompt — Follow-Up Agenda
Based on this meeting's discussion and outstanding items, suggest an agenda for a follow-up meeting with time estimates.
ℹ️

Custom Recaps: Choose Speaker Summary, Executive Summary, or create your own template — e.g., "Research Meeting Notes" with methodology updates, data collection status, next steps.

Notebooks & Pages — Smart Workspaces

Copilot Notebooks brings together all relevant materials and works on them with Copilot in a scoped workspace. Copilot Pages turns any response into a collaborative, editable canvas you can share.

📓 Notebooks

Centralize all materials for a project. Add docs, slides, sheets, meeting notes, and chats. Copilot analyzes only your curated content.

📄 Pages

Turn any Copilot response into a collaborative page. Edit, expand, and share via Teams or Outlook.

🎓 Study Guide

Transform learning materials into an interactive study guide with summaries, Flashcards, and Quizzes.

Prompt — Research Synthesis
Based on all materials in this notebook, identify common themes, contradictions between sources, and gaps. Create a synthesis for a literature review foundation.
Prompt — Create a Page
Create a page summarizing research methodology options for [topic]. Include a comparison table of qualitative vs. quantitative approaches with pros and cons.

Researcher & Analyst — Advanced AI Agents

Two groundbreaking agents (GA since mid-2025). They perform multi-step research and analysis, think iteratively, and return deep, sourced results.

🔍 Researcher

Powered by OpenAI's deep research model. Multi-step research across org data and web with cited reports. Supports Salesforce, Confluence connectors.

📈 Analyst

Powered by o3-mini reasoning model. Analyzes data, runs Python in real time. View the code and check each step.

Prompt — Researcher: Field Survey
Research the current state of [field/topic] — recent developments, key debates, leading researchers, methodological trends. Use web and org files. Produce a structured report with cited sources.
Prompt — Researcher: Funding Trends
Analyze funding trends in [area] over 5 years. Emerging priorities? Most active funders? Identify opportunities for our group based on our published work in /[folder].
Prompt — Analyst: Data Exploration
Analyze /[filename.xlsx]. Perform EDA, test normality, run correlation analysis, create visualizations for significant findings. Show the Python code.
Prompt — Analyst: Survey Results
Analyze survey results in /[survey_data.xlsx]. Calculate Cronbach's alpha, perform factor analysis, generate demographic breakdowns. Create publication-ready tables and charts.
💡

Where to find them: Microsoft 365 Copilot → Agents → select Researcher or Analyst.

Prompt Library for Academic Researchers

Click any prompt to copy it. Replace [brackets] with your own details.

📚 Literature Review & Initial Research

▼
Field Mapping
I'm starting a literature review on [topic]. Help me identify key theoretical frameworks, seminal works, and current debates. Organize by: (1) foundational theories, (2) major research streams, (3) trends, (4) gaps.
Source Comparison
Compare methodological approaches in /[paper1.pdf] and /[paper2.pdf]. Create a table: research design, sample size, data collection, analysis techniques, limitations.
Research Gaps
Based on /[notes], identify significant research gaps. For each: why it matters, what methodology could address it, potential contribution.
Article Summary
Summarize /[article.pdf]: (1) Research question, (2) Theory, (3) Methodology, (4) Findings, (5) Limitations, (6) Future research. Max 500 words.

✍️ Academic Writing

▼
Abstract
Write a structured abstract (300 words max) for a [quant/qual/mixed] study on [topic]. Include: Background, Objective, Methods, Results, Conclusion.
Introduction
Draft an introduction for a paper on [topic]. Use the "funnel" structure: broad field, specific problem, gap, research questions. ~800 words.
Methodology
Draft a methodology section for a [type] study. Include: design, participants (N=[n]), instruments, procedure, analysis, ethics. Target journal: [name].
Discussion
Based on /[results.docx], draft a discussion: (1) main findings, (2) literature interpretation, (3) implications, (4) limitations, (5) future research.
Language Improvement
Improve academic writing quality: precision, clarity, transitions, remove redundancy, scholarly language, consistent terminology. Preserve original argument.

📊 Data Analysis

▼
EDA
Perform EDA: missing values, descriptive statistics, outliers, distributions, normality tests. Suggest appropriate statistical tests.
Survey Analysis
Analyze survey data: (1) response rates, (2) reliability (Cronbach's alpha), (3) frequency tables, (4) cross-tabulations, (5) visualizations.
Visualization
Create publication-quality charts: (1) bar chart comparing [groups], (2) line graph of trends over [time], (3) scatter plot of [var1] vs [var2]. Clear labels and clean design.
Data Cleaning
Clean dataset: handle missing values, remove duplicates, standardize dates, fix errors. Keep a change log for the methodology section.

📨 Academic Communication

▼
Journal Cover Letter
Draft a cover letter for submitting "[title]" to [journal]. Highlight novelty, relevance, key findings. State it hasn't been submitted elsewhere.
Rebuttal Letter
Point-by-point response to reviewers. For each: (1) quote original comment, (2) our response, (3) changes with page/line references. Professional tone.
Collaboration Email
Email to [Professor] at [University] proposing collaboration on [topic]. Introduce myself, explain synergy, suggest framework, propose meeting. Formal tone.

🏫 Teaching & Mentoring

▼
Course Syllabus
Create a syllabus for a [level] course on [topic]. Include: description, Bloom's taxonomy objectives, weekly schedule, assessment, integrity policy.
Discussion Questions
From /[reading], generate 8 graduate seminar questions at comprehension, analysis, evaluation, synthesis levels. Add 2 connecting to current issues.
Student Feedback
Constructive feedback for a student's proposal on [topic]. Address: research question, lit review, methodology, feasibility, writing. Be encouraging and specific.
Exam Creation
Create an exam for [course] from /[slides.pptx]. 10 MCQs (with key), 3 short-answer, 1 essay. Cover all topics at varying difficulty.

📁 Administration & Organization

▼
Progress Report
From recent emails, meetings, and documents on [project], draft a quarterly report: objectives achieved, activities, challenges, budget, next quarter plans.
Activity Summary
Summarize my past month's meetings, emails, and documents for [department]. Main decisions, pending items, approaching deadlines.
Recommendation Letter
Draft a recommendation for [student] applying to [program] at [institution]. We worked on [project]. Strengths: [list]. One page, warm and professional.

Quick Reference — Tips & Shortcuts

/ — Reference a File

Type / in any Copilot prompt to select a specific file. Works with Word, PowerPoint, Excel, SharePoint, and OneNote.

🔄 Iterate & Refine

Don't settle for the first response. Ask Copilot to improve, shorten, expand, or change tone — every conversation is iterative.

📌 Pages — Save Good Output

Great response? Turn it into a Page — edit, expand, and share with others.

📓 Notebooks — Per Project

Create a Notebook for each research project. Gather materials and ask focused questions.

🎭 Personas

Assign a role: "As a statistical consultant...", "As a journal editor..." — responses will be more targeted.

🛡️ Always Verify!

Check facts, citations, and statistics before using any output. Professional responsibility is yours.
